Main Menu

Significance
The main menu is generally the first thing you see upon starting a game, but you don't generally spend much time there. As such, it is of average significance
Relevance
Standard main menu; average relevance.
Score
Laser-pointer menu

Ingame Menus

Significance
Ingame menus include everything from pause menus to radial menus for quick actions. As these are interacted with more, these are of higher significance than the main menu
Relevance
Level select / preferences, average relevance
Inventory, high relevance
Score
Laser pointer preferences/levels menu
Picky radial menu

HUD

Significance
Traditional HUDs are less prevelant in VR; as such, this is generalized to however important information is protrayed; Health, ammo, etc.
Relevance
Shows critical information (Ammo, health)
Score
Health is shown through a vignette/eye closing and audio cues
Ammo is not very visible; oftentimes requiring you to open the menu to check how much you have left.

Controls

Significance
Controls refers to basic actions such as locomotion. This usually comes in the form of controller bindings.
Relevance
Includes locomotion; super relevant
Score
Thumbstick is often neccesary to be used when climbing to force the character to move in the right direction
Crouching is done via thumbstick; Actually crouching does not work, as the game assumes you're transitioning to seated mode
Vehicle movement is controlled via thumbstick

Immersion

Relevance
First-person game; immersion is super relevant

Presence

Significance
Presence describes the physical presence of the player in the world. (The character model)
Relevance
Fully physics-based avatar, super relevant

Hands

Significance
Hand presence covers your hands, whether you have hands, floating controllers, or nothing at all.
Relevance
Humanoid character, very relevant
Score
Very well done, detailed hands in all avatars, including on the main menu

Upper Body

Significance
Upper body presence includes arms, chest, and head.
Relevance
Includes physics-based climbing; super relevant
Score
Detailed upper body, arm tracking even adjusts to the avatar.

Full Body

Significance
Full body presence includes arms, legs, and feet
Relevance
Physics-based climbing, jumping, etc. Super relevant
Score
Detailed full body presence, but IK is not great; legs often point outwards at extreme angles, especially when climbing
When seated in a vehicle, leaning even slightly can fold your body in half, sometimes requiring you to exit and reenter the vehicle to fix it. (which isn't possible in the minecart)

Interactions

Significance
Interactions are the main immersive part of VR; this includes self-interactions, interacting with items, weapons, props in the world, and static objects
Relevance
Physics-based world; interactions are super relevant

Self

Significance
Self interaction includes any and all interactions with yourself, without holding items or weapons
Relevance
Full body presence; physics-based world. Super relevant.
Score
Multiple pockets for storing items, body log on arm.
Tracking adjusts to proportions of current avatar to prevent clipping
specific pockets are often difficult to access, especially the back pocket

Tools

Significance
Tool interaction includes interactions with weapons and tools. Tools include any item that serves a useful purpose
Relevance
Guns and swords and whatnot; super relevant
Score
Weapons are very nice to use and guns are quick to reload
Two-handing some weapons such as pistols works very poorly, and often prevents you from pointing straight

Props

Significance
Props are any object you could expect to pick up, both light and heavy. This includes functional items that may not serve a useful purpose, such as markers
Relevance
Lots of props all around the world, super relevant
Score
Everything is interactible, and often destructible as well; very well done.

World

Significance
World interaction includes interaction with any world elements that are not static. This means doors, drawers, breakable windows, and other objects that can be interacted with, but not picked up.
Relevance
Very populated world; many elements all around. (This includes vehicles)
Score
Most world interactions are well done; lockers, etc.
Doors can be opened by hitting them, very satisfying.
Crane controls in the lab are very poor, grabbing them makes them harder to control and you oftentimes lift yourself up rather than moving the levers
Go-kart steering behavior is poor
Mounted guns are very difficult to aim, almost unusable

Static

Significance
Static interaction involves interactions with static objects such as walls and fixed furniture
Relevance
Physics-based world; super relevant
Score
Static interactions are generally good, but oftentimes collisions are bouncy; climbing up walls can launch you away, etc

Locomotion

Significance
Locomotion is an important part of immersion, as it provides continuity when moving from one place to another
Relevance
Parkour and physics-based world; super relevant
Score
Sprinting is slow to respond
Climbing is very finicky and often results in the player being launched away from ledges when free-climbing (when you're not holding onto something)
Jumping is inconsistent, sometimes jumps higher than others, sometimes leaves you crouching
