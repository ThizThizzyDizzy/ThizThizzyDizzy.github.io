VR Reviews: Scoring

I review VR games on their VR-ness! But what does that mean?
I do not review games based on their story, mechanics, or even by how much I like the game.
Instead, games are scored based on how well they adapt to the medium of VR itself.
Here's some scores:

Half-Life: Alyx

The maximum score describes the best score the game could achieve based on how relevant each category is to the game.
A game's score describes how well implemented each category is, weighted by its relevance.
The score is split into 5 main categories:

Graphics

Graphics covers the amount of detail present in shaders, textures, and other elements.

Audio

Audio covers music, ambience, and other sound effects.

Interface

Interface covers menus, HUD elements, and basic controls.

Immersion

Immersion covers the most immersive parts of gameplay. This includes interactions, locomotion, and presence.

Hardware

Hardware covers support for various hardware features, from controller haptics to full-body tracking

Each of these categories may be split into sub-categories. Details will depend on the specific game in question.
Some games will have a Mods section on the main score. This includes all score increases due to mods in all categories.
